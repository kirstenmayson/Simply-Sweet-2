Chocolate Swiss roll

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs separated
  • 15 grams castor sugar
  • 40 grams cake flour
  • 10 grams cocoa powder
  • 35 grams Milk
  • 2 grams baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • 25 grams vegetable Oil
  • 3 grams Vanilla extract
  • 50 grams sugar

For the filling and topping

  • 250ml fresh cream
  • 150ml milk
  • 1 Packet of chocolate mousse pudding
  • 125ml fresh cream
  • 50 grams dark chocolate

Recipe:

  1. Preheat the oven to 170°C
  2. Separate eggs. Chill the egg whites in the freezer for 5 minutes
  3. Whisk together the three egg yolks, 15 g sugar and salt until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the 35 g milk, 25 g vegetable oil and vanilla extract. Whisk.
  5. Sift in the 40 g cake flour, 10 g Coco powder and baking powder into the egg yolk mixture. Whisk, set a side.
  6. Beat the egg whites until bubbles start to form then add in the 50 g of sugar a little at a time. Continue to beat until stiff peaks start to form.
  7. Place 1/3 of the egg whites into the chocolate mixture and fold in with a whisk until well combined. Add the chocolate mixture into the rest of the egg whites and fold in until well combined.
  8. Pour the mixture into a 37.5 cm x 25.4 cm tin covered in baking paper. Tap the baking pan down on the table to release the bubbles and bake in a preheated oven at 170°C for 18 minutes.
  9. Cool and make the filling.

For the filling

  1. Beat together the one packet of chocolate mousse pudding as well as the 250 mL fresh cream. When it is starting to get thick add the 150 mL milk a little at a time and beat until creamy and light.
  2. Cut a big enough piece of baking paper and place it on the counter. Turn the Swiss roll out of the tin and place on the baking paper.(To avoid it sticking, dust some icing sugar on top of the swiss roll before flipping it onto the piece of baking paper.)
  3. Spread the chocolate mousse mixture onto the chocolate Swiss roll and roll it.
  4. Top with fresh cream and grated chocolate. Let it set in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
